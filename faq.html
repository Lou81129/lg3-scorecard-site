Printing

Flip on SHORT side. Always. Forever. No exceptions. This is the One True Setting™ for LG3 scorecards.

If you choose “flip on long side,” the back will be upside‑down, sideways, or spiritually misaligned.

Because the scorecards are printed two‑up in landscape, which means the natural binding edge is the short edge. Flip on long side tells the printer to rotate the back like it’s printing a novel.

Unless you want your scorecards to read like a mystery thriller, stick with short side.

Auto‑Duplex: The printer flips the paper internally and prints both sides automatically. Canon MF663Cdw does this beautifully.

Manual Two‑Pass: The printer prints the front, then asks you to flip and reinsert the paper. This is the HP 6830 lifestyle.

If your printer pauses and says “Load page for second side,” that’s manual two‑pass. If it doesn’t pause, that’s auto‑duplex. If it prints only one side, that’s your printer being dramatic.

Canon MF663Cdw: Use the multi‑purpose tray for cardstock or custom sizes. Auto‑duplex works from this tray.

HP OfficeJet Pro 6830: Use the main tray. It cannot auto‑duplex custom sizes and requires manual two‑pass.

If you try to force cardstock through the HP duplex path, it will respond by making noises that sound expensive.

Yes. The LG3 scorecard cardstock is flexible enough to run through the default tray on both Canon and HP printers. It’s not rigid poster board — it feeds smoothly.

You can still use the multi‑purpose tray if you prefer, but it’s not required.

Canon’s internal duplex path is designed to handle heavier paper, custom sizes, and multi‑purpose tray feeds without complaining. It flips cleanly, centers consistently, and respects your settings.

Canon = predictable. HP = moody but usable with the right settings.
