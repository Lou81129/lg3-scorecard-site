LG3 · Game Card — Tampa Palms G&CC — Back Side
USGA Rules of Golf apply except where modified by the LG3 Local Rules below.

• Root Relief — Free relief if a root or large rock affects swing, stance, or safety.

• Wildlife Relief — Free relief from gators, snakes, or any unsafe wildlife interactions.

• Embedded Ball Relief — Through the green, free relief.

• Cart Path Relief — Free relief when the path affects lie, stance, or swing.

• Divot Relief — Free relief from a sanded or unfilled divot.

• Safety Relief — Free relief at nearest safe point, no closer to the hole.

• Gimmies — Inside the leather (33–34 in). Add one stroke and pick up.

• Hole 18 Water Hazard — If your ball enters the water hazard on Hole 18, you may take a drop on the opposite side of the hazard (right of the cart path). The drop point must be no closer to the hole and equidistant from the pin to where your ball last crossed the margin of the hazard.

• Posting to GHIN — All players must post their score to GHIN after the round. If you used mulligans (especially on Par 3s), you must add those strokes back to your final score before submitting to GHIN.

LG3 MATCH PLAY — LOCAL RULES

• Format — Two-man Best Ball. Each player plays their own ball. Lowest NET score on each team wins the hole.

• Handicaps — Strokes fall where they fall. Dots applied per selected method.

• Picking Up — A player may pick up once out of the hole. Record the score they would have reasonably made.

• Concessions — Putts, holes, and the match may be conceded at any time. A conceded putt is considered holed.

• Order of Play — Honor goes to the team that won the previous hole. Ball farthest from hole plays first after tee shots.

• Halved Holes — If both teams tie NET, the hole is halved (H).

• All Holes Must Be Played — Even if match ends early, all 18 holes must be completed for tiebreak purposes.

• Out of Bounds / Lost Ball — Stroke-and-distance applies. The hole is not automatically lost.

• No Provisionals — If lost or OB, replay from the previous spot with one penalty stroke.

• Unplayable Ball — One penalty stroke using any of the three standard options.

• Loose Impediments — May be removed anywhere without penalty. Ball must not move.

• Movable Obstructions — May be removed without penalty. Replace ball if it moves.

• Water Hazards — One penalty stroke: play as it lies or take standard relief.

• Advice — You may advise your partner but not your opponents. No physical assistance with stance or swing.

SCRAMBLE RULES (TEAM FORMAT)

• Four player USGA Scramble

• All players tee off → team selects the best shot → all players play from that spot → repeat until holed

• One team score per hole (Gross & Net)

• Team Handicap applied using USGA recommended method

• Minimum 3 tee shots per player must be used

• Place ball within 1 club length of the selected shot (same cut of grass)

• On the green: place ball within 6 inches, no closer to the hole

• In bunkers: rake and place the ball after selecting the shot

• Penalty areas: all players drop in the same relief area

• Lost ball / OB: proceed under USGA rules using the selected shot as reference

• Once the ball is holed, no further strokes may be played

• Scramble rounds are NOT posted to GHIN (USGA rule)

CHAPMAN (PINEHURST) FORMAT RULES

• Two-player teams — Team A (Players 1 & 2) vs Team B (Players 3 & 4)

• Both players tee off on every hole

• Each player then hits their partner's tee shot (second shot)

• After the second shot, the team selects the best ball and plays alternate shot into the hole

• One team score per hole (Gross & Net)

• Team Handicap: (60% × Low CH) + (40% × High CH)

• Strokes fall on holes ranked by HDCP — lowest HDCP numbers receive strokes first

• Scoring is Match Play — holes won (W), lost (L), or halved (H) vs opposing team

• Concessions: putts, holes, and the match may be conceded at any time

• All 18 holes must be completed even if the match is decided early
